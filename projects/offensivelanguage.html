Offensive Language Detection

  • Tech Stack: Python, NLP, Scikit-learn, Stanford CoreNLP

Overview: This project tackles the rising challenge of harmful online behavior, including offensive language and cyberbullying, on social media platforms. By combining robust text preprocessing, feature extraction, and machine learning techniques, it provides a system capable of identifying and mitigating harmful content, especially targeting adolescent safety.

Problem Statement: Cyberbullying and exposure to offensive content negatively impact mental and emotional health, especially among teenagers. The anonymity provided by social media platforms exacerbates the issue, enabling harmful behavior without fear of repercussions. This study aims to address these challenges by detecting and categorizing offensive language patterns efficiently.

Key Techniques:

  • Data preprocessing to remove stopwords, punctuation, and non-alphabetic characters for cleaner analysis.
  • Feature extraction using TF-IDF vectorization with unigrams, bigrams, and trigrams to capture contextual relevance.
  • Integration of a Negative Use Rate (NUR) metric to enhance identification of offensive terms.
  • Classification using a Linear Support Vector Machine (SVM) for precise binary categorization of posts as offensive or neutral.
  • Performance evaluation using metrics like ROC AUC, F1 Score, and Precision-Recall AUC.

Key Contributions:

  • Introduction of a comprehensive preprocessing pipeline tailored for offensive language detection.
  • Focus on harmful terms frequently associated with cyberbullying and their contextual usage.
  • Development of a balanced dataset emphasizing underrepresented offensive content.
  • Analysis of model performance across multiple datasets to ensure generalizability.

Impact: The system contributes to safer online spaces by automatically identifying harmful content, supporting efforts to combat cyberbullying and offensive language. Its application can extend to real-time moderation tools for social media platforms.

Research Insights: The study highlights the pervasive nature of offensive language online and its correlation with cyberbullying. The findings emphasize the importance of targeted detection algorithms and suggest pathways for further refinement, including deeper linguistic analysis and exploration of additional abusive categories.
